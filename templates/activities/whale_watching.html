Whale Watching

During Humpback Whale season, generally Nov - April, we will take several trips out to watch the whales. It is truly amazing and you will see many whales and likely will get up close and personal with them. I can't count how many times we've had whales come up next to the boat and under the boat. Bring a large memory card for you camera during these trips!

Many times while watching for whales, we will encounter spinner dolphins which love to come by the boat and put on a show for you! Anytime during the day is a good time to go out, as the whales are active all day. However, as the day gets later, the winds and current get stronger which makes for a choppy trip in the afternoon.

We highly recommend using the Pacific Whale Foundation for your whale watching trips (and snorkel trips too!). The PWF has two locations, one in Ma'alaea and the other in Lahaina. Here's a link to their website: www.pacificwhale.org

During whale season you will also have no problems spotting whales from the shore, sometimes just a few hundred yards from the shore. So while you're sitting back relaxing on the beach, keep an eye out on the water for whales!

Another great way to whale watch is from a kayak or stand up paddle boards. Each can be rented from different places in Kihei, especially the Kalama Park area. During whale season, you don’t have to go out far to spot the whales and I’ve even had them go right underneath me!
